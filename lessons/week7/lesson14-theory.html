Lesson 14: Relationship Phrases

Назария Машқҳо Видео

Objective

Learn and practice phrases to describe relationships, with a focus on military-related contexts. Students will be able to describe relationships using appropriate vocabulary and grammar, and apply these phrases in conversations.

Омӯзиш ва машқи ибораҳои муносибат, бо диққати махсус ба контексти ҳарбӣ. Донишҷӯён метавонанд муносибатҳоро бо истифодаи луғат ва грамматикаи мувофиқ тавсиф кунанд ва ин ибораҳоро дар муколамаҳо истифода баранд.

Word Pronunciation Example Тарҷума

Grammar Point

Using "is" to describe relationships

Learn how to use the verb "is" to describe relationships in the present tense:

  • He is my comrade. (Ӯ ҳамрадаи ман аст.)
  • She is my fellow soldier. (Ӯ ҳамрадаи ҳарбии ман аст.)
  • They are my teammates. (Онҳо ҳамдастагони ман ҳастанд.)
  • He is my squad leader. (Ӯ сардастаи ман аст.)

Activities

1. Practice Describing Relationships

Work with a partner and take turns describing your relationship using the key vocabulary:

A: "He is my squad leader." (Ӯ сардастаи ман аст.)
B: "She is my fellow soldier." (Ӯ ҳамрадаи ҳарбии ман аст.)

2. Role-Play: Introduce a Colleague or Teammate

Practice these questions with a partner:

  • "Who is your squad leader?" (Сардастаи шумо кӣ аст?)
  • "Is he your comrade?" (Оё Ӯ ҳамрадаи шумо аст?)
  • "She is my fellow soldier. What is her role?" (Ӯ ҳамрадаи ҳарбии ман аст. Вазифаи Ӯ чист?)

3. Create a List of Phrases

Work in groups to create a list of 10 relationship phrases using the key vocabulary and grammar:

  • "He is my teammate." (Ӯ ҳамдастаи ман аст.)
  • "She is my comrade." (Ӯ ҳамрадаи ман аст.)
  • "They are my squad members." (Онҳо аъзои дастаи ман ҳастанд.)

Homework

  • Write a short paragraph about your military unit using relationship phrases.
  • Create a diagram of your unit and label each member using the key vocabulary.

Assessment

  • Oral Assessment: Participation in role-play and relationship descriptions
  • Written Assessment: Sentences and paragraphs about relationships
